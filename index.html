Advance tax & interest rules
  • Who: anyone whose net tax liability (after TDS) is ₹10,000+ in the year. Resident senior citizens with no business/professional income are exempt (Sec 207).
  • When: 15% by 15 Jun · 45% by 15 Sep · 75% by 15 Dec · 100% by 15 Mar (cumulative).
  • 234C: 1% per month for 3 months on each deferred installment (1 month for the March one). No interest on the first two if you'd paid at least 12% / 36% respectively.
  • 234B: if total advance tax paid is under 90% of the liability — 1% per month (or part) on the shortfall from 1 April 2026 until you pay.
  • 234A (not computed here): a further 1% per month applies only if you also file the return after the due date with tax unpaid.
  • Let-out property rent counts toward advance tax liability — TDS u/s 194-IB (if your tenant deducts it) counts as tax already paid.
Switching rules you should know
  • The new regime is the default. To use the old regime, you must actively opt out.
  • Salaried / no business income: choose the regime every year directly in ITR-1 or ITR-2 — no separate form needed. File before the Section 139(1) due date.
  • Business or professional income: opting out requires Form 10-IEA before the due date, and you get only one lifetime switch back to the new regime.
  • Tell your employer your intended regime for correct TDS — but that intimation alone doesn't count as opting out; the choice is exercised in the ITR itself.
